## Pattern Discovery: Fragmentation vs. Consolidation

**Winner pattern: Fragmented markets > Consolidated markets**

| Market | Buyer Count | Best Score | Why |
|--------|-------------|------------|-----|
| **Broadcast** | 3,400+ | 8.5 | Fragmentation = peer comparison |
| **Telecom** | 3 | 7.4 | Consolidation = no virality |
| **Healthcare** | 200K+ providers | 8.8-9.2 | Extreme fragmentation |
| **Architecture** | 30K+ firms | 8.9-9.1 | Fragmentation = benchmarking |
| **RIAs** | 13K+ | 8.4-8.7 | Fragmentation = peer anxiety |

**Insight:** **Consolidated markets kill virality.** When there are only 3-5 potential customers:
- No peer benchmarking (everyone knows each other's data)
- No competitive anxiety (no secrets)
- Professional networks don't exist (everyone already knows everyone)
- Procurement cycles are long and relationship-based

**Fragmented markets enable:**
- Peer benchmarking FOMO ("How are peers doing this?")
- Anonymous sharing ("We found a better way")
- Professional associations (natural distribution)
- Faster procurement decisions (no relationship required)
