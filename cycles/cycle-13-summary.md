## Key Insights

### Why Global Regulatory Arbitrage Works

**1. US Market Focus**:
- US startups focus on domestic markets (300M+ population, homogeneous regulations)
- Regional markets viewed as "too small" despite significant size
- US startups can't navigate language, regulatory, cultural barriers

**2. Local Credential Moats**:
- Professional licenses (statutory auditors, architects, securities lawyers)
- Regulatory approvals (national/provincial authorities)
- Language capabilities (French, German, Japanese, Arabic, Portuguese)

**3. Regulatory Urgency**:
- EU CSRD: Phase 1 deadlines already passed (companies panicking)
- Japan: 2025 seismic retrofitting deadline + labor shortage
- UAE: Mandatory AI certifications by 2025-2026
- Canada: 2024 climate disclosure rules
- Brazil: 2024 enforcement phase with massive fines

### The Arbitrage Opportunity

**Our Advantage**: Focus on ONE region deeply vs. US startups' shallow global approach
- Build regulatory moat in ONE country → expand regionally
- Become the "local expert" → US startups become customers/partners
- Acquire local credentials → barrier to entry for foreign competitors
