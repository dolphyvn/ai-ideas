## Climate/ESG: Lessons Learned

### What Works (8.0+)

1. **Immediate financial pain** - Fines that hurt NOW, not future reporting
2. **Sub-regional scope** - States/cities, not global/national
3. **Fragmented buyers** - Lots of small customers vs. few large
4. **Municipal data** - Local ordinances create data moat
5. **Clear compliance owner** - Single role responsible

### What Doesn't Work (<8.0)

1. **Global ESG reporting** - Incumbents (Workiva, SAP) dominate
2. **Annual/biannual reporting** - Low urgency, procrastination possible
3. **Commodity markets** - Carbon credits, RECs price to zero
4. "Save the planet" framing - Financial ROI must be primary
5. **Scope 3 supply chain** - Data doesn't exist, impossible to verify

### The "Climate Trap"

**Many climate ventures fail because:**

| Trap | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Virtue signaling** | Buyers purchase for PR, not utility | Carbon offsets |
| **Reporting fatigue** | Too many frameworks, ignore all | ESG reports |
| **Green shipping** | Willingness-to-pay near zero | Green routing (5% premium max) |
| **Consulting dressed as tech** | Implementation requires humans | CSRD materiality |
| **Data impossible** | Trying to measure what doesn't exist | Scope 3 supplier data |

**Key insight:** Climate tech must solve a REAL problem (fines, compliance cost, operational efficiency) not a PERCEIVED problem (green image).
