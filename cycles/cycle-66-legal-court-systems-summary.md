## Cycle 66 Insights

### Why Legal Court Systems Failed (Incumbent Fortress Pattern):

**Legal Tech Consolidation:**

| Category | Incumbents | Market Power |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Practice Management** | Clio, PracticePanther | Already have customer data + workflows |
| **Legal Research** | LexisNexis, Westlaw, Fastcase | Already have court integrations |
| **E-Discovery** | Relativity, Everlaw, Logikcull | Embedded workflow features |
| **Docket/Calendar** | Integrated into practice suites | Feature, not product |

**Why Legal Tech = Incumbent Fortress:**
1. **Practice management systems already exist** - Clio has 150,000+ users
2. **Court integrations are expensive** - Only incumbents can afford
3. **Feature creep** - Every practice tool adds deadline tracking eventually
4. **Enterprise sales cycles** - 6-12 month legal department procurement
5. **Relationship-driven** - Lawyers trust established brands

**This matches Telecom pattern:**
- Consolidated incumbents with deep relationships
- Can add AI features in 6 months
- Data moats controlled by incumbents
- Long sales cycles
