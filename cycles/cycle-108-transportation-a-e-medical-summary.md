## Signal 1: Pilot Certificate & Medical Tracking (200K pilots)

### Idea 1: AeroMed Guardian AI

**Concept:** AI-powered FAA medical certificate tracking and renewal platform for pilots and flight departments. Monitors medical expiration, predicts disqualifying conditions before AME exams, and automates the SI/SSPI waiver process.

**Market Size:**
- Total Addressable Market: 200K pilots (150K private, 50K commercial)
- Serviceable Addressable Market: 80K pilots with active medical certificates
- Enterprise buyers: 5K flight departments, charter operators, flight schools

**Practice-Blocking Urgency:**
- FAA medical certificate lapse = cannot fly (practice-blocking)
- Commercial pilots: lose $5K-15K/week when grounded
- Flight departments: lose $50K+/week per pilot out of service
- Medical expires every 6-60 months depending on age/class

**Business Model:**
- Enterprise: $1,500/month per flight department (up to 10 pilots)
- Enterprise+: $4,000/month per flight department (unlimited pilots)
- Individual: $29/month per pilot

**Year 3 Revenue Potential: $18M ARR**

**AI Advantage:**
1. **Predictive Condition Screening**: AI analyzes pilot health data against FAA disqualifying conditions list before AME visits
2. **SI/SSPI Wafer Automation**: LLMs generate waiver documentation from medical records
3. **AME Finder**: NLP-based matching of pilots to medical examiners based on condition expertise
4. **Expiry Prediction**: ML models predict medical deferral risk based on age, conditions, medication changes

**Moat:**
- Proprietary database of 3,000+ FAA AME specialization patterns
- FAA medical form parsing (MedXPress is complex PDF-based)
- Historical deferral/success rates by condition
- Integration with FAA Pilot Records Database (PRD)

**Key Metrics:**
- CAC: $500 (enterprise)
- LTV: $18,000 (enterprise, 12-month retention)
- LTV/CAC: 36x
- Monthly churn: 3%

**Primary Risks:**
- FAA regulatory approval for PRD access
- Pilot privacy concerns (medical data sensitivity)
- AME resistance to AI-generated documentation
- Low individual pilot adoption (price sensitivity)

**Sources:**
- FAA Airmen Certification Statistics 2025
- FAA Aviation Medical Examiner Directory
- AOPA Pilot Medical Resource Guide
- NBAA Flight Department Operations Report
