## Name

**TitleIXComplyAI - DOE Title IX Investigation & Compliance Automation Platform**

---

## Core Concept

AI-powered platform for higher education Title IX coordinators and offices that automates case intake and classification, investigation timeline management, documentation and evidence tracking, DOE report generation, and provides risk prediction based on complaint patterns. Uses ML for case risk assessment trained on DOE OCR database outcomes.

---

## Problem Statement

### The Regulatory Burden

- **4,000+ Title IX-covered institutions** in the US (2,000 degree-granting + 2,000 non-degree)
- **DOE investigations increased 400%** since 2021
- **Average investigation cost: $250K-$750K** per case
- **Loss of federal funding** = institutional death for some institutions
- **2024 proposed regulations** create additional complexity and requirements

### The Title IX Requirements

```
Title IX Requirements (2020 updates + 2024 proposed):
- Title IX Coordinator required (1-3 FTE depending on size)
- Grievance procedures for sex discrimination
- Prompt and equitable resolution
- Supportive measures for both parties
- Documentation and record retention
- Public availability of rights and policies
- Training for mandatory reporters
- Clery Act overlap (crime reporting)
- DOE investigation response (30-90 day windows)
- Annual reporting to DOE
- Public posting of training materials

Enforcement:
- DOE can withhold federal funding
- Public investigation lists (DOE OCR database)
- Title IX coordinator can be held personally liable
- Civil rights investigations
- Expensive legal defense
```

### The Investigation Reality

| Investigation Finding | Consequence | Impact |
|----------------------|-------------|--------|
| **Failure to have procedures** | DOE findings, remediation plan | Public |
| **Inadequate response** | Resolution agreements, monitoring | Expensive compliance |
| **Systemic violations** | Multiple cases, monitoring for years | Oversight |
| **Retaliation** | Separate violation, double damages | Liability |

### The Title IX Coordinator Experience

```
"I received 187 complaints last year. I have one part-time assistant.
Our tracking is spreadsheets that don't talk to each other. We were
put under DOE investigation for 'inadequate response.' The investigation
took 14 months. Cost us $450K in legal fees plus $150K in consultants.
We avoided a finding but just barely. I don't sleep before every
OCR update. I'm one mistake away from losing my job or worse."
- Title IX Coordinator, Mid-sized University
```
