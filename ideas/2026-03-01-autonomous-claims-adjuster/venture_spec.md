# AI Venture Spec

## Name:
**Autonomous AI Claims Adjuster Network**

## Core Concept:
Network of AI agents that autonomously handle property insurance claims from first notice to settlement. Assesses damage from photos/videos, estimates repair costs, approves/rejects payments, and negotiates with contractors—reducing cycle time from 30-60 days to hours.

## Problem:
- **150K adjuster jobs unfilled by 2027**: 30% of workforce retiring
- **$120B+ annual catastrophe losses**: Climate-driven surge
- **30-60 day cycle times**: Customer satisfaction crisis
- **30% of premiums = claims expense**: $300B+ annual optimization target
- **Fraud leakage**: $80B+ in fraudulent claims paid annually

## Target Vertical:
**Primary**: Property & Casualty Insurance Carriers
- Mid-market carriers ($100M-$5B revenue)
- Auto insurers (glass, fender benders first)
- Homeowners insurers (weather damage)
- Specialty carriers (appliance, electronics)

**Secondary**: Managing general agents (MGAs), third-party administrators (TPAs)

## Why Now:
1. **Multimodal AI breakthrough**: Can assess damage from smartphone photos
2. **Agent orchestration maturity**: Multi-agent systems can negotiate autonomously
3. **Computer vision advances**: Detects fraud patterns humans miss
4. **Edge AI capability**: Instant decisions at the scene
5. **Regulatory acceptance**: States allowing AI-based auto claims (telematics)

## AI Advantage:
- **Photo/video damage assessment**: Computer vision estimates repair costs
- **Fraud detection**: Pattern recognition across millions of claims
- **Contractor negotiation**: Agents negotiate with repair shops autonomously
- **Instant decisions**: Edge AI enables real-time approvals
- **Document processing**: Extracts data from police reports, estimates, invoices

## Viral Mechanism:
- **Carrier FOMO**: Competitors processing claims in hours vs. 30 days
- **Customer pressure**: Insureds demand faster settlements
- **Industry conferences**: Claims conferences share results
- **Case studies**: "Reduced cycle time 95%" spreads fast

## Revenue Model:
**Per-Carrier Subscription:**
- **Starter**: $50K/year - Up to 10K claims/month
- **Growth**: $200K/year - Up to 50K claims/month
- **Enterprise**: $500K/year+ - Unlimited + white-label

**Performance Pricing:**
- 10-20% of claims cost savings

**Implementation:**
- $100K one-time for enterprise (integration, training)

**Unit Economics:**
- CAC: $150K (enterprise sales, conferences)
- LTV: $1.5M (36-month avg retention)
- Gross Margin: 80%

## Moat:
1. **Regulatory approvals**: State insurance commission requirements
2. **Data network effects**: More claims = better fraud detection
3. **Integration depth**: Core systems integration (Guidewire, Duck Creek)
4. **Domain expertise**: Insurance rules + fraud patterns + repair costs
5. **Agent orchestration**: Multi-agent coordination complexity

## MVP in 12 Weeks:
**Weeks 1-4:**
- Focus: Auto glass + minor fender benders
- Photo-based damage assessment
- Repair cost estimation

**Weeks 5-8:**
- Partner with 2 mid-size carriers
- Pilot on 5% of low-complexity claims
- Measure: Accuracy target 95%+

**Weeks 9-12:**
- Expand to simple homeowners claims
- Contractor negotiation agent
- Regulatory outreach (state approvals)

**Validation Metrics:**
- Cycle time reduction: 60 days → 3 days (95% reduction)
- Accuracy: 95%+ agreement with human adjusters
- Carrier saves: $500K+ in pilot
- 2+ carriers commit to rollout

## Tech Stack:
- **Backend**: Python/FastAPI
- **AI**: GPT-4V (damage assessment), Claude 4.6 (negotiation)
- **Computer Vision**: YOLO, custom damage detection models
- **Agents**: LangGraph for multi-agent orchestration
- **Infrastructure**: AWS (ECS, Bedrock, S3 for photos)

## Monthly Revenue Potential (Year 1–3):
- **Year 1**: $250K MRR (6 carriers × $40K ARPU)
- **Year 2**: $1.5M MRR (30 carriers + enterprise expansion)
- **Year 3**: $5M MRR (100 carriers + performance revenue)

## Risk Factors:
1. **Regulatory resistance**: State insurance commissions may block AI-only claims
2. **Liability exposure**: Wrong assessment = legal action
3. **Incumbent response**: Guidewire, Salesforce adding AI features
4. **Adoption friction**: Carriers are risk-averse
5. **Fraud arms race**: Fraudsters adapt to AI patterns

## Competitive Threat:
- Guidewire (legacy system, adding AI)
- Salesforce (CRM, not claims-native)
- Snapsheet (photo-based, limited workflow)
- Mitchell (manual processes, tech debt)

**Differentiation**: Fully autonomous end-to-end, multi-agent negotiation, fraud detection
