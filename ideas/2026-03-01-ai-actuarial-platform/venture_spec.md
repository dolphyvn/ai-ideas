# AI Venture Spec

## Name:
**AI Actuarial Modeling Platform**

## Core Concept:
Automated actuarial workflow platform for property/casualty insurance companies. Handles loss reserving, pricing models, catastrophe risk analysis, and regulatory reporting—replacing manual spreadsheets with AI-driven automation.

## Problem:
- **80% of actuaries retiring by 2030**: Catastrophic talent shortage
- **Manual spreadsheets**: Most actuarial work still done in Excel
- **Catastrophe risk surge**: Climate disasters causing $100B+ annual losses
- **Social inflation**: Jury awards inflating 30%+ annually
- **Regulatory complexity**: NAIC, state departments demanding more sophisticated modeling

## Target Vertical:
**Primary**: Mid-market P&C Insurance Carriers
- Regional carriers ($100M-$5B revenue)
- Specialty lines (professional liability, cyber, agriculture)
- Captive insurance units

**Secondary**: Reinsurance companies, MGAs, large self-insured corporations

## Why Now:
1. **Climate risk urgency**: Hurricanes, wildfires driving massive losses
2. **Talent crisis**: 80% of actuaries retiring, inadequate pipeline
3. **AI capability**: Can now model complex catastrophe scenarios
4. **Regulatory sandboxes**: NAIC states allowing AI-based reserving
5. **Social inflation**: Traditional models breaking down

## AI Advantage:
- **Loss reserving automation**: AI analyzes claims data to predict reserves
- **Catastrophe modeling**: Climate change scenarios, geographic risk
- **Pricing optimization**: Dynamic pricing based on real-time data
- **Regulatory reporting**: Auto-generates NAIC annual statements
- **Risk scoring**: Social inflation, litigation trends, economic factors

## Viral Mechanism:
- **Industry conferences**: Casualty Actuarial Society (CAS) presentations
- **Regulatory approvals**: State department endorsements = trust signal
- **Case studies**: "Reduced reserve error by 40%" spreads fast
- **Carrier referrals**: Mid-market carriers share results

## Revenue Model:
**Per-Carrier Subscription:**
- **Regional**: $250K/year - Single line of business
- **Multi-line**: $750K/year - Multiple LOBs, advanced modeling
- **Enterprise**: $2M/year - Full suite, custom models, on-premise option

**Implementation:**
- $150K one-time (data migration, model training, state filings)

**Unit Economics:**
- CAC: $200K (actuarial conferences, direct sales)
- LTV: $3M (36-month retention, massive switching costs)
- Gross Margin: 80%

## Moat:
1. **Regulatory moat**: State insurance department approval required
2. **Credential requirement**: FCAS/FSA actuaries must validate
3. **Data moat**: Carrier claims data improves models
4. **Switching costs**: Replacing actuarial systems is 18+ month projects
5. **Talent barrier**: Need actuaries to build, scarce resource

## MVP in 16 Weeks:
**Weeks 1-4:**
- Focus: ONE line of business (workers comp)
- ONE mid-market carrier pilot
- Manual loss reserving analysis

**Weeks 5-8:**
- Build AI reserving model (claims history + inflation)
- Regulatory report generation (NAIC templates)
- State filing preparation

**Weeks 9-12:**
- Deploy pilot with friendly carrier
- Validate: Reserve accuracy within 5% of manual
- State regulatory outreach

**Weeks 13-16:**
- Add catastrophe modeling (climate scenarios)
- Expand to 2 more carriers
- Pricing optimization module

**Validation Metrics:**
- Reserve error <5% vs. manual
- 2+ carriers commit after pilot
- State regulatory approval received
- $10M+ in reserves modeled accurately

## Tech Stack:
- **Backend**: Python/FastAPI, R (actuarial models)
- **AI**: Custom ML models (XGBoost, neural nets)
- **Data**: Snowflake (claims data), Databricks (modeling)
- **Infrastructure**: AWS (Sagemaker, EC2)

## Monthly Revenue Potential (Year 1–3):
- **Year 1**: $350K MRR (15 carriers × $23K ARPU)
- **Year 2**: $1.75M MRR (75 carriers)
- **Year 3**: $5M MRR (250 carriers + reinsurance)

## Risk Factors:
1. **Regulatory resistance**: States may reject AI-based reserving
2. **Liability exposure**: Wrong reserves = insolvency risk
3. **Talent dependence**: Need actuaries for validation
4. **Incumbent response**: Milliman, Towers Watson adding AI
5. **Model risk**: AI black boxes concern regulators

## Competitive Threat:
- **Milliman**: Consulting firm, building AI tools
- **Towers Watson**: Software + consulting, legacy systems
- **Verisk**: Catastrophe modeling leader, adding AI
- **Insurity**: Legacy actuarial software

**Differentiation**: Purpose-built AI platform, not consulting add-on. Full workflow automation, not point solutions.
