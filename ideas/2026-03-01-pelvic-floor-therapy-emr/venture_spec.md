# AI Venture Spec

## Name:
**PelvicTrack - Pelvic Floor Therapy EMR**

## Core Concept:
Specialized EMR for pelvic floor physical therapists focusing on women's health, men's pelvic health, and complex insurance billing. Handles sensitive documentation, specialized assessments, and biofeedback/e-stim billing.

## Problem:
- **Zero dedicated software**: Pelvic floor PTs use paper or modify general PT platforms
- **Sensitive documentation**: Pelvic exams require privacy many general EMRs lack
- **Complex billing**: Biofeedback, e-stim, multiple anatomical systems
- **Growing market**: 1 in 4 women (25%) have pelvic floor dysfunction
- **Underserved**: Only 3,000-5,000 specialists vs. 300,000+ general PTs

## Target Vertical:
**Primary:** Pelvic Floor Physical Therapists
- Women's health specialists (prenatal/postpartum, incontinence)
- Men's pelvic health (post-prostatectomy, pelvic pain)
- Private practice owners (have purchasing autonomy)

**Secondary:** Urogynecology practices, colorectal surgeons (referrals), OB-GYNs

## Why Now:
1. **Growing awareness**: Postpartum care, women's health destigmatized
2. **Aging population**: Pelvic floor disorders increase with age
3. **Insurance acceptance**: ACA mandates improved coverage
4. **Biofeedback adoption**: More insurers covering e-stim/biofeedback
5. **PT EMRs inadequate**: General platforms lack pelvic-specific tools

## AI Advantage:
- **Sensitive documentation**: PPDI, PFDI-20 questionnaires, pelvic grading scales
- **Assessment tools**: Range of motion, muscle grading, outcome measures
- **Billing optimization**: CPT codes for biofeedback (90901), e-stim, manual therapy
- **Privacy first**: Extra security for sensitive patient information
- **Outcome tracking**: Quality of life measures, functional improvement metrics

## Viral Mechanism:
- **Specialist conferences**: Section on Women's Health (APTA), CAPP
- **Referral networks**: OB-GYNs, urogynecologists refer patients
- **Patient outcomes**: Word-of-mouth in women's health communities
- **Case studies**: "Improved outcomes 40%" in pelvic health spaces

## Revenue Model:
**Per-Therapist Subscription:**
- **Solo**: $200/month - Single practitioner
- **Practice**: $500/month - Up to 5 therapists
- **Clinic**: $1,200/month - Unlimited + custom forms + biofeedback integration

**Per-Patient add-on:**
- $10 per patient complex documentation package

**Implementation:**
- $2,500 one-time (data migration, assessment tools, training)

**Unit Economics:**
- CAC: $100 (specialist conferences, referral networks)
- LTV: $5,400 (36-month retention)
- Gross Margin: 85%

## Moat:
1. **Privacy barrier**: Sensitive documentation creates natural moat
2. **Specialized assessments**: Pelvic-specific tools not in general EMRs
3. **Niche market**: Too small for Epic/Cerner to care
4. **Treatment complexity**: Biofeedback, e-stim, manual techniques
5. **Licensing**: CAPP-PF certification requires training

## MVP in 12 Weeks:
**Weeks 1-3:**
- Focus: Women's health (incontinence, pelvic pain)
- Manual assessment tool creation
- Privacy-first documentation system

**Weeks 4-6:**
- Build AI documentation generator (PPDI, PFDI-20)
- Outcome tracking (quality of life scores)
- Biofeedback billing optimization

**Weeks 7-9:**
- Partner with 3 private practices
- Deploy pilot
- Measure: Documentation time reduction, billing accuracy

**Weeks 10-12:**
- Add men's pelvic health module
- E-stim documentation
- Prepare APTA conference presentation

**Validation Metrics:**
- 50%+ documentation time reduction
- 95%+ billing acceptance rate
- 3+ practices commit after pilot
- Patient outcomes trackable

## Tech Stack:
- **Backend**: Python/FastAPI
- **AI**: GPT-4 (documentation), Custom ML (outcome prediction)
- **Security**: HIPAA compliant + additional privacy safeguards
- **Infrastructure**: AWS (US regions, encrypted at rest)

## Monthly Revenue Potential (Year 1–3):
- **Year 1**: $20K MRR (200 therapists × $100 ARPU)
- **Year 2**: $100K MRR (1,000 therapists)
- **Year 3**: $300K MRR (3,000 therapists + clinic licenses)

## Risk Factors:
1. **Market size**: Limited specialist pool (3,000-5,000)
2. **Adoption resistance**: Some therapists prefer paper
3. **Billing complexity**: Payers may not recognize specialized codes
4. **Liability**: Sensitive patient data requires extra security
5. **Regulation**: HIPAA + state privacy laws

## Competitive Threat:
- **WebPT**, **TheraBill**: General PT platforms, not pelvic-specific
- **Clinicast**: Documentation platform, not pelvic assessments
- **HelloNote**: Mental health focused, not pelvic floor
- **General EMRs**: Lack sensitivity and specialization

**Differentiation**: Only EMR designed specifically for pelvic floor therapy with privacy-first architecture and specialized assessments.

## Exit Path:
- **WebPT/TheraBill**: Acquisition to add pelvic health module
- **HelloDoc**: Mental health platform expanding to physical therapy
- **Women's health platforms**: Maven, Tia (for integration)
- **APTA**: Section on Women's Health endorsement
