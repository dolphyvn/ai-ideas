# AI Venture Spec

## Name:
**Multi-Party Legal Collaboration Platform**

## Core Concept:
A "QuickBooks for legal" platform enabling real-time collaboration between enterprise legal teams and external law firms in a unified system. Solves the $800B legal tech market's biggest gap: multi-player workflow.

## Problem:
- **$200B/year on external counsel**: Zero coordination tools
- **Single-player tools only**: Harvey, Ironclad serve one side
- **Massive friction**: Email, PDFs, disjointed workflows
- **Network effects lost**: No cross-organization learning
- **Legal tech gap**: Every industry has collaboration tools except legal

## Target Vertical:
**Primary**: Mid-market companies ($10M-500M revenue)
- Tech companies managing outside counsel for commercial contracts
- Manufacturing with multiple law firms (IP, litigation, employment)
- Healthcare systems (regulatory, compliance, litigation)

**Secondary**: Mid-market law firms seeking competitive edge

## Why Now:
1. **Harvey validation**: $80B valuation proves legal AI demand
2. **Harvey is single-player**: Gap for multi-party solution
3. **Enterprise collaboration maturity**: Figma, Notion, Slack set expectations
4. **Legal spend pressure**: Companies cutting external counsel spend
5. **AI capability**: Long-context models can handle complex matters

## AI Advantage:
- **Cross-org workflow**: Real-time collaboration on matters
- **Document intelligence**: Contract analysis, IP portfolio tracking
- **Matter prediction**: Outcome forecasting based on historical data
- **Network analytics**: Learn from anonymized cross-org data
- **Compliance tracking**: Multi-jurisdictional regulatory monitoring

## Viral Mechanism:
- **Law firm referrals**: "Use this platform to work with us"
- **Legal operations community**: Share best practices
- **Network effects**: More users = smarter platform
- **Case studies**: "Cut external counsel spend 30%"

## Revenue Model:
**Per-Organization Subscription:**
- **Company**: $5K/month - Up to 5 external firms
- **Law Firm**: $3K/month per client company connection
- **Enterprise**: $25K/month - Unlimited + white-label + API

**Implementation:**
- $10K one-time for enterprise (migration, training, integrations)

**Unit Economics:**
- CAC: $100K (enterprise sales, legal conferences)
- LTV: $900K (36-month retention)
- Gross Margin: 85%

## Moat:
1. **Network effects**: Each new firm creates value for all companies
2. **Workflow data**: Cross-org collaboration graphs impossible to replicate
3. **Switching costs**: Multi-org coordination = lock-in
4. **Integration depth**: Core legal systems (Clio, iManage, NetDocuments)
5. **Data moat**: Anonymized outcome predictions improve with use

## MVP in 12 Weeks:
**Weeks 1-4:**
- Focus: Commercial contract collaboration
- One tech company + one law firm pilot
- Document sharing + version control + AI summarization

**Weeks 5-8:**
- Real-time collaboration (comments, tasks, approvals)
- Matter tracking + spend analytics
- Expand to 3 more company-firm pairs

**Weeks 9-12:**
- Network analytics (anonymized benchmarking)
- Compliance workflows (conflicts, privilege)
- Pricing refinement

**Validation Metrics:**
- 5+ company-firm pairs active
- 30%+ reduction in email/PDF friction
- External counsel spend reduction measurable
- 2+ law firms adopting for all clients

## Tech Stack:
- **Backend**: Python/FastAPI
- **AI**: Claude 4.6 (contract analysis), GPT-4o (drafting)
- **Collaboration**: Real-time sync (WebSocket), CRDTs for documents
- **Integrations**: Clio API, iManage, DocuSign, Adobe Sign
- **Infrastructure**: AWS (ECS, S3, PostgreSQL)

## Monthly Revenue Potential (Year 1–3):
- **Year 1**: $150K MRR (20 companies + 15 law firms)
- **Year 2**: $750K MRR (100 companies + 75 law firms)
- **Year 3**: $2.5M MRR (350 companies + 250 law firms)

## Risk Factors:
1. **Incumbent response**: Harvey, Ironclad adding collaboration
2. **Sales cycle**: Legal tech sales are 12-18 months
3. **Law firm resistance**: Some firms prefer opaque billing
4. **Data security**: Cross-org data sharing concerns
5. **Adoption friction**: Lawyers resist new tools

## Competitive Threat:
- **Harvey**: AI contract review, single-player only
- **Ironclad**: CLM, not multi-party
- **DocuSign**: Signature focused, not collaboration
- **Clio**: Practice management, not cross-org

**Differentiation**: Multi-party collaboration is the gap. No incumbent solves end-to-end workflow across organizations.

## Exit Path:
- **Harvey**: Natural acquirer ($80B valuation, need collaboration)
- **Ironclad**: Expanding beyond CLM
- **DocuSign**: Beyond signatures
- **Microsoft**: Legal tech consolidation
