## Core Concept

AI-powered monitoring platform for aviation professional certificates. Tracks pilot currency (BFR, medical, flight review, instrument currency), mechanic certificate currency, and enforcement actions. Scrapes FAA airmen database, sends proactive alerts, and generates "ready-to-fly" status reports for operators.

**The Bridge:** Manual Currency Tracking (spreadsheets, logbook reviews) → [AI] → Automated Certificate Monitoring (FAA data scraping, proactive alerts)

---

## Problem Statement

### The Certificate Currency Crisis

- **200K+ FAA-certified pilots** in the US
- **Flying with expired currency** = violations, insurance denial, certificate action
- **Operators managing 20+ pilots** - manual tracking spreadsheets error-prone
- **Medical certificates** every 12-60 months - missed dates ground pilots
- **BFR every 24 months** - flight reviews easily missed

### The Current Reality

```
"We manage 35 pilots across our charter operation. Twice last year
we discovered a pilot had expired instrument currency AFTER they'd
flown revenue trips. We're lucky FAA didn't find out first. Our
director of safety spends hours each month manually checking pilot
certificates. We'd pay $500/month for automated monitoring that
actually catches this before it becomes a violation."
- Director of Operations, Part 135 Charter Operator
```

## Why Now

### 1. Public FAA Data Available

- FAA airmen database is publicly queryable
- Certificate status, ratings, expirations accessible
- No proprietary integration required

### 2. SMS Requirements Expanding

- FAA Part 5 SMS requirements expanding to smaller operators
- Certificate tracking is SMS prerequisite
- Regulatory tailwind increasing urgency

### 3. Dual-Sided Market Opportunity

- Individual pilots willing to pay for peace of mind
- Operators willing to pay more for crew compliance
- Enterprise pricing subsidized by volume

### 4. Incumbent Gap

- Logbook apps exist but don't pull official FAA data
- No incumbent monitors certificates in real-time
- Boeing/Airbus focus on airlines, not GA/Part 135
