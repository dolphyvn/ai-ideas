## Core Concept

AI-powered Airworthiness Directive (AD) compliance platform for general aviation and Part 135 operators. Automatically ingests FAA AD feeds, matches to aircraft fleets, tracks compliance deadlines, and generates required documentation. Uses NLP to parse AD language and extract actionable requirements.

**The Bridge:** Manual AD Tracking (email subscriptions, spreadsheets) → [AI] → Automated Compliance Monitoring (FAA RGL parsing, fleet matching, deadline tracking)

---

## Problem Statement

### The AD Compliance Crisis

- **200K+ registered US aircraft** - each subject to ADs
- **Missing AD compliance** = grounded aircraft, FAA violations, insurance denial
- **FAA issues 200+ ADs annually** - tracking manually overwhelming
- **Fleet operators** managing multiple makes/models - complex matching
- **Compliance deadlines** range from immediate to 12+ months

### The Current Reality

```
"We operate 18 aircraft across 4 different types. Keeping up with
every AD is a full-time job for one of our mechanics. Last year we
missed an AD on a Citation because it was buried in a monthly email.
We only discovered when FAA ramp inspection found it. $25K fine
plus we had to ground the aircraft for 2 weeks. We'd pay $500/month
for AI that actually tracks this properly."
- Director of Maintenance, Part 135 Charter Operator
```

## Why Now

### 1. Public FAA Data Available

- FAA Regulatory Guidance Library (RGL) has all ADs back to 1940
- Email subscription feeds available
- No proprietary integration required

### 2. Compliance is Non-Negotiable

- ADs are mandatory airworthiness requirements
- Missing AD = aircraft unairworthy
- FAA ramp checks increasingly common

### 3. Fleet Complexity Growing

- Operators managing multiple aircraft types
- Make/model/engine combinations create complexity
- Manual tracking impossible at scale

### 4. Incumbent Gap

- Boeing/Airbus serve airlines with integrated systems
- GA/Part 135 underserved by modern compliance tools
- Legacy solutions are clunky, expensive
