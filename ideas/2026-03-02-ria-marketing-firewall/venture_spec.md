## Core Concept

AI-powered marketing compliance platform for RIAs. Monitors websites, social media, emails, and marketing materials for SEC Rule 206(4)-1 violations. Uses ML trained on SEC marketing enforcement actions to detect prohibited testimonials, misleading performance claims, and improper fee disclosures.

**The Bridge:** Manual Marketing Review (error-prone, slow) → [AI] - Real-Time Compliance Monitoring (catch violations before publication)

---

## Problem Statement

### The Marketing Compliance Crisis

- **SEC Rule 206(4)-1** has 30+ specific prohibitions
- **Social media scrutiny increasing** - SEC monitoring LinkedIn, Twitter
- **Marketing enforcement up 60%** since 2021
- **Fines: $50K-250K per violation** - can be multiple per incident
- **One bad post = enforcement** - viral risk
- **Manual review impossible** - content published faster than review

### The Current Reality

```
"Our advisers post on LinkedIn and Twitter constantly. We can't
review everything before it goes live. Last month, an adviser
shared a client testimonial in a comment - SEC violation. We're
paying fines and updating policies. We'd pay $1,500/month for AI
that monitored all our marketing and caught violations before
publication."
- CCO, Mid-Market RIA
```

## Why Now

### 1. SEC Marketing Rule Enforcement

- SEC issued 100+ marketing enforcement actions in 2023
- Priority areas: testimonials, performance claims, fee disclosures
- Social media a key focus area

### 2. Content Explosion

- RIAs publishing content daily (blogs, social media, videos)
- Gen X/Y advisers embracing digital marketing
- Review can't keep up with publication velocity

### 3. Viral Risk

- One problematic post goes viral → enforcement
- Reputational damage spreads instantly
- Fines can exceed marketing ROI

### 4. No Real-Time Solutions

- Existing tools are review-after-publication
- No real-time social media monitoring
- GPT wrappers lacking enforcement pattern training
