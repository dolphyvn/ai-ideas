# Validation Plan: PortDetentionAI

## Hypothesis
Food importers will adopt a $0/month + $25K activation crisis insurance model for FDA detention protection because each prevented detention saves $100K-1M in cargo losses and supply chain disruption.

## Validation Method: Concierge MVP

### Week 1: Manual Service Test
**Action:**
- Identify 20 high-volume fresh produce importers (100+ containers/month)
- Cold email logistics directors with offer: "Free detention risk assessment - we'll analyze your shipment history"
- Manually analyze their manufacturers against FDA OAI refusal databases
- Deliver report: "12 of your 87 manufacturers have elevated detention risk"

**Success Metric:**
- 5/20 importers reply interested
- 2 importers agree to paid pilot

### Week 2-3: Landing Page + Waitlist
**Action:**
- Launch simple landing page (Vercel + Next.js)
- Value prop headline: "Stop Losing $100K-1M to FDA Detention"
- CTA: "Get Free Detention Risk Assessment" + "Join Waitlist"
- Run LinkedIn ads targeting produce/seafood importers
- Post in trade association groups (United Fresh, NFI) with value-first content

**Success Metric:**
- 75 waitlist signups from importers
- 25 customs brokers sign up for partnership info
- 10 importers request full risk assessment

### Week 4: Pilot Program
**Action:**
- Onboard 2-3 importers into manual concierge service
- Weekly report: "Here are your shipments with elevated detention risk this week"
- Include: manufacturer risk score, specific FDA refusal history, recommended mitigation
- Track: Do they adjust orders? Any detentions prevented?

**Success Metric:**
- Importer reports at least 1 avoided detention
- Importer willing to sign $0/month + $25K activation agreement
- Net Promoter Score > 8
- Logistics director becomes champion (refers 1+ peer)

- 2 importers sign crisis insurance agreement
- 20+ importers on waitlist

- Clear evidence of prevented detentions

**Go/No-Go Decision (Week 4):**

## Pilot Design Details

### Target Importer Profile
- 100+ containers/month (high volume)
- Perishable products (produce, seafood, dairy)
- Imports from 5+ countries (diversified supply chain)
- Has experienced FDA detention in past 12 months (pain awareness)

### Pilot Deliverables
- **Weekly Risk Report**: Manufacturers with elevated detention risk
- **Shipment Scoring**: Detention probability for each shipment
- **Petition Guarantee**: 48-hour petition if detention occurs
- **FDA Liaison**: Direct communication with FDA district office

### Pilot Pricing
- Free 30-day monitoring pilot
- $0/month ongoing, $25K activation if detention occurs
- Month-to-month, no commitment

## Pivot Options if Validation Fails

**Pivot 1: Subscription-First Model**
- If crisis insurance doesn't convert, test $999/month subscription with petitions included
- Value prop: "Unlimited petition generation + risk monitoring"
- Target: Mid-size importers with consistent detention issues

**Pivot 2: Customs Broker-Only Strategy**
- Pivot to selling exclusively through brokers as white-label solution
- Higher volume, lower touch distribution
- Broker-facing product, not importer-facing

**Pivot 3: Specialization Pivot**
- Narrow to single high-risk category (e.g., berries from Mexico/Central America)
- Deep expertise in specific product-origin corridors
- Higher accuracy, narrower market

**Pivot 4: Insurance Company Partnership**
- Partner with cargo insurance providers
- Bundle detention protection with cargo insurance policies
- B2B2C model through insurance distribution

## Customer Discovery Questions

### For Importers:
1. How many FDA detentions did you experience in the past year?
2. What was the total value lost to detentions?
3. How long does it currently take you to respond to a detention notice?
4. Who handles FDA petitions internally? What's their process?
5. Would you pay $25K to save a $150K shipment? What about $50K?
6. How do you currently assess detention risk before booking shipments?
7. What would better prediction enable you to do differently?

### For Customs Brokers:
1. How often do your clients experience FDA detention?
2. What do clients do when detained? Do you help?
3. Would you offer detention protection to clients if available?
4. What revenue share would make this compelling for you?
5. How do you currently communicate detention risk to clients?
6. What integration would make this seamless for your workflow?

### For Trade Associations:
1. Is FDA detention a top concern for your members?
2. What resources do you currently provide on this topic?
3. Would you promote a solution that saves members $100K+ per incident?
4. What member discounts would drive adoption?
