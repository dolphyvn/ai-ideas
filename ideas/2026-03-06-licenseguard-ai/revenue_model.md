## Revenue Streams

### Primary: Hospital System Subscription (80% of revenue)
- **Expected mix:** 70% medium/large hospitals, 30% small hospitals
- **Churn target:** <5% annually (high switching costs)
- **Expansion revenue:** 20%+ annual upsell (additional modules, more physicians)

### Secondary: Telemedicine Platform Partnerships (15% of revenue)
- **Model:** Per-physician monthly fee based on active count
- **Growth driver:** Telemedicine expansion (25% CAGR)
- **Integration value:** Embedded in credentialing workflows

### Tertiary: Individual Physician Subscriptions (5% of revenue)
- **Model:** B2C subscription
- **Growth driver:** Hospital-sponsored accounts (zero marginal cost)
- **Marketing channel:** Doximity, physician social media

### Add-on: Emergency Lapse Recovery
- **Model:** $0/month monitoring, $25,000 emergency fee
- **Trigger:** When license lapses and 48-hour expedited renewal needed
- **Service:** Emergency board coordination, expedited processing, document preparation
- **Margin:** 70% (we keep $17.5K, pay $7.5K to expedited service partners)

## Pricing Rationale

### Why Hospitals Will Pay $150K-800K/year
1. **Alternative cost**: Hospitals spend $2K-5K/year per physician on manual credentialing/compliance
2. **Revenue protection**: One lapse = $50K+/week in lost revenue per physician
3. **Crisis prevention**: Credentialing crises damage hospital reputation, patient trust
4. **Staff efficiency**: Credentialing departments are understaffed, overwhelmed
5. **Liability reduction**: Disciplinary risk prediction prevents compliance incidents

### Why Telemedicine Platforms Will Pay $200-400/month per Physician
1. **Onboarding blocker**: License verification is first step in credentialing
2. **Multi-state requirement**: Telemedicine requires multistate licenses
3. **Automated monitoring**: Ongoing compliance required for platform participation
4. **Competitive differentiation**: Faster credentialing = more physicians on platform

### Why $199 for Individuals
- Low enough for impulse purchase (high-income professionals)
- High enough to imply value
- Most individuals will be hospital-sponsored (zero marginal cost)
- Target segment: telemedicine physicians, locum tenens, independent contractors

## Pricing Iteration Strategy

### Start High (Value-Based Pricing)
- Hospital budgets are large ($2K-5K/year per physician for compliance)
- License lapse costs $50K+/week per physician
- Pricing anchored to value delivered, not cost-plus

### Volume Discounts
- Larger hospitals get lower per-physician pricing
- Telemedicine platforms get volume-based tiers
- Multi-year contracts get 10-20% discount

### Geographic Pricing
- No geographic variation (national pricing)
- California, Texas, Florida (largest states) same pricing
- Simplifies sales, reduces pricing friction

- Disciplinary risk prediction (+$100K/year)
