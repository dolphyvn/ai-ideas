# Validation Plan: LicenseGuard AI

## Hypothesis
Hospital credentialing departments will pay $150K-800K/year for automated physician license tracking because each prevented lapse saves $50K+/week in lost revenue and credentialing crises.

## Validation Method: Concierge MVP

### Week 1: Manual Service Test
**Action:**
- Identify 10 mid-sized hospital systems (200-500 physicians each)
- Cold email credentialing directors with offer: "Free license audit - we'll find your expiring licenses this month"
- Manually scrape public physician license databases for their physicians
- Deliver report: "23 of your 347 physicians have licenses expiring in 90 days"

**Success Metric:**
- 3/10 hospitals reply interested
- 1 hospital agrees to paid pilot

### Week 2-3: Landing Page + Waitlist
**Action:**
- Launch simple landing page (Vercel + Next.js)
- Value prop headline: "Stop Losing $50K/Week to Expired Physician Licenses"
- CTA: "Get Free License Audit" + "Join Waitlist"
- Run LinkedIn ads targeting hospital credentialing directors
- Post in NAMSS (National Association Medical Staff Services) groups

**Success Metric:**
- 30 waitlist signups from hospital systems
- 10 waitlist signups from individual physicians
- 5 hospitals request full audit

### Week 4: Pilot Program
**Action:**
- Onboard 1-2 hospital systems into manual concierge service
- Weekly email: "Here are your physicians with licenses expiring in next 90 days"
- Include: renewal requirements, CME gaps, direct links to state renewal portals
- Track: Did they act on alerts? Any lapses prevented?

**Success Metric:**
- Hospital reports at least 1 prevented lapse
- Hospital willing to pay $150K+ for annual subscription
- Net Promoter Score > 8
- Credentialing director becomes champion (refers 1+ peer)

## Success Criteria

**Minimum Viable Signal (Week 4):**
- 1 hospital system pays $150K+ for annual contract
- 10+ hospital systems on waitlist
- Clear evidence of prevented lapses
- Credentialing director champion established

**Go/No-Go Decision (Week 4):**
- ✅ GO: 2+ paying hospitals OR 30+ waitlist with strong intent
- ❌ NO-GO: 0 paying hospitals AND <15 waitlist

## Pilot Design Details

### Target Hospital Profile
- 200-500 employed physicians
- Multi-state presence (physicians licensed in 3+ states)
- Active credentialing department (2-5 full-time staff)
- Telemedicine program or growing multistate practice

### Pilot Deliverables
- **Weekly Report**: Physicians with licenses expiring in 90 days
- **CME Gap Analysis**: State-specific CME shortages identified
- **Renewal Checklist**: Step-by-step requirements for each expiring license
- **Disciplinary Flags**: Physicians with elevated malpractice claim activity

### Pilot Pricing
- Free 30-day pilot (no commitment)
- $150,000/year for first 200 physicians if converted
- Option to expand after 6 months

## Pivot Options if Validation Fails

**Pivot 1: Direct-to-Physician B2C**
- If hospitals won't pay, test $199/year subscription with individual telemedicine physicians
- Value prop: "Never lose a day's income to license lapse again"
- Target: Telemedicine physicians with 3+ state licenses

**Pivot 2: Compliance-as-a-Service**
- Full-service compliance: we handle renewals end-to-end
- Higher price point ($500K-1M/year), higher touch
- Focus on large health systems with 500+ physicians

**Pivot 3: Specialty-Specific Focus**
- Narrow to high-risk specialties (anesthesiology, surgery, emergency medicine)
- Higher urgency, higher pricing power
- Specialty-specific CME tracking (e.g., cardiac CME for cardiologists)

**Pivot 4: Telemedicine Platform Play**
- Pivot from hospitals to telemedicine platforms as primary customer
- API-first approach, embed credentialing into platform workflows
- Volume pricing based on active physicians on platform
