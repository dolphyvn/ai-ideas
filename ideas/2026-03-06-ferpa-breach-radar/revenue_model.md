## Pricing Rationale

### Why Institutions Will Pay $75K-250K/year

1. **Penalty avoidance:** $57,500 per FERPA violation (civil penalty)
2. **DOE monitoring:** Enhanced monitoring for 2+ years creates operational burden
3. **Federal funding risk:** Title IV funding = institutional survival
4. **Manual cost:** Breach scope assessment takes 2-3 weeks of FTE time (~$10-15K per breach)
5. **Insurance requirement:** Cyber insurance increasingly requires breach detection

### ROI Calculation (Mid-Size University)

**Annual Costs:**
- FERPA Breach Radar: $150,000/year
- Implementation (one-time): $25,000

**Annual Savings:**
- Breach scope labor: 2 breaches/year × $15,000 = $30,000
- Potential penalties: 1 prevented breach × $57,500 = $57,500
- DOE monitoring avoidance: $100,000/year (consulting, reporting)
- Cyber insurance discount: $20,000/year
- **Total savings: $207,500/year**

**Net ROI:** ($207,500 - $150,000) / $150,000 = **38% first-year ROI**

### Pricing Psychology

- **Anchored to penalties:** $150,000 vs. $57,500 per violation (3 violations = payback)
- **Deadline urgency:** 120-hour clock creates "must have" vs. "nice to have"
- **Insurance parallel:** Cyber insurance premiums $50-200K/year - familiar budget line
- **Staff cost alternative:** 1 FTE security analyst = $150-200K/year (salary + benefits)
