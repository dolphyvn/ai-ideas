## Core Concept

AI-powered enforcement intelligence platform that ingests FDIC/OCC/NCUA consent orders, extracts required corrective actions and timelines, and generates predictive compliance insights. The system identifies patterns across enforcement actions to help banks proactively address gaps before their own exams—using 100% public data.

**The Bridge:** Reactive Enforcement Response (consent orders = business restrictions) → [AI] → Predictive Compliance Intelligence (peer patterns as early warning)

---

## Problem Statement

### The Consent Order Crisis

- **5,000+ banks and credit unions** face enforcement risk
- **2023 bank failures** (SVB, Signature, First Republic) increased exam scrutiny
- **Consent orders carry**: Business restrictions, civil penalties, reputational damage, board removal
- **Remediation costs**: $1M+ for typical consent order
- **Peer enforcement patterns**: Regulators use consistent findings across institutions

### The Current Reality

```
"When our peer bank received a consent order for BSA/AML deficiencies,
our board panicked. We had similar gaps but didn't know until our
competitor's enforcement became public. We spent $2M and 18 months
remediating under regulator supervision. If we'd had AI that analyzed
public consent orders and flagged our exposure, we would have paid
$50K annually for that early warning."

```

## Why Now

### 1. 2023 Bank Failures Aftermath

- SVB, Signature, First Republic failures = industry-wide exam scrutiny
- Regulators applying lessons learned across all institutions
- Enforcement actions increasing 40%+ post-2023

### 2. All Consent Orders Are Public

- FDIC, OCC, NCUA publish enforcement actions
- Structured data: findings, deadlines, penalties, remediation requirements
- No proprietary data required for analysis

### 3. Competitive Fear Creates Urgency

- "Did you see what happened to [peer]?" drives immediate action
- Benchmarking against peer enforcement creates FOMO
- Boards asking "Are we exposed to the same issues?"

### 4. Community Banking Underserved

- Enterprise compliance tools priced at $100K+
- Community banks need $15K-50K solutions
- Compliance consultants serve as distribution channel
